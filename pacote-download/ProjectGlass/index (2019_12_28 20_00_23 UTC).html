Learn more about Google Glass

By Diego Dias

Updated on the July 14th of 2019

What is it?

The Google Glass is an accessory in the format of glasses that allows the user interaction with diverse contents in augmented reality. Also called Project Glass , this device is capable of taking pictures with voice commands, sending instant messages and performing video files with hotfixes. Its launch is scheduled for 2022, and its price could be around $1,500. Glass Glass is currently in the testing phase and already there is a video fully recorded with the device. In addition, the search company has registered new anti-theft patents and screen unlocking for the accessory.

Launch date

There is no specific and official date for the device to be released yet. It may be available demos by the beginning of 2020, but its release for stores is at least for 2022.

How does it works?

According to trustful sources close to Google, the glasses will have a small LCD or AMOLED screen at the top and in front of the user's eyes. Camera and GPS which you can tag yourself, as well as excelent options that follow head movements

What can you do with Google Glass?

Google's ad video shows that you can be turned into a kind of "super-human," since the device can indicate how many yards you are from your destination, whether the subway is open or closed, show the weather forecast, notes and even allow you to schedule meetings with only voice commands.

Glass news

Google has finally released the complete specifications of Google Glass, and along with it a surprise never ever seen in the market: the search giant will use an audio system based on driving transduction. Through the stems of the glasses, the sound will be transmitted to the user's ear through microvibrations in certain bones of their head, without using any type of speaker.

Additionally, the screen placed at the front of the user's eye also caught attention. There will be 640 x 360 pixels of resolution that proportionally would be 25-inch high definition monitor placed 2.5 meters away from the user.
